COVID-19 Driving Stock Market Higher Than 1 Year Ago?

Adam Novotny 2020-05-16
share share

COVID-19 became a serious concern for wider public in the USA somewhere between February and March of 2020. Today on May 16, stock markets in the USA are higher than a year ago while most of the country is still under strict lockdown restrictions.

Uncertainty around the virus is still high and vaccine seems to be 12–18 months away. The closest region approaching herd immunity seems to be NYC with 20% but that is nowhere near herd immunity. “Experts predict at least 70 percent of the population will need to be immune to the virus in order to achieve herd immunity.”

Most economics charts worldwide have “broken” websites because y-axis do not reach low enough. So negative has the virus impact been on everyday life.

Bloomberg Global PMI: 39.8

JPMorgan Chase’s snapshot of the health of manufacturing around the world

Bloomberg U.S. Employment: −20,537K

Bloomberg U.S. consumer spending: −7.5%

Bloomberg German Ifo: 74.3

The leading indicator of health in the euro area’s largest economy based on a survey of ~7,000 executives in German manufacturing, services, retail, wholesale and construction companies.

How does the stock market optimistic behavior make any sense?

There are only 2 unconvincing answers that come to mind:

  • Stock markets are forward looking and all the damage of the virus is behind us.
  • FED can support the markets indefinitely by buying everything with printed money. The following chart shows the FEDs assets which have broken the “y-axis” in the opposite direction current sitting at $6,934,227,000,000, or $6.9T

The first explanation suggest that there is a lot of brave people betting against the virus. The second suggests that future inflation will never become a factor. Neither is convincing.
