Book Notes: Everything Explained for the Professional Pilot by Richie Lengel

2020-09-19

While summarizing this book isn’t useful due to its technical nature, below is a small subset of fun facts I came across. I am not listing items by importance. Everything in the book is important, and the relatively importance depends on the stage of pilot’s flight training.

  • GPS calculates position based on precise signals received from satellites. How precise? Being 1/1000 of a seconds off results on in a 186-mile error in location. Main GPS system has 24 satellites, 4 of which must reach device at a time to calculate 3D position + time. They orbit Earth at 11k miles twice a day
  • Frequency ranges: Broadcast radio 535–1,605 kHz (wavelength 560–187m). Communications, VOR, marker beacons 30–300MHz (wavelength=10–1m). Transponder, radar, glidescope, GPS 300–3,000 MHz (wavelength 100–10cm)
