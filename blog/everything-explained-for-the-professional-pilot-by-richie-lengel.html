Everything Explained for the Professional Pilot by Richie Lengel

2020-09-19

While summarizing this book isn’t useful due to its technical nature, below is a small subset of fun facts I came across.

  • GPS calculates position based on precise signals received from satellites. How precise? Being 1/1000 of a second off results in a 186-mile error in location. Main GPS system has 24 satellites, 4 of which must reach device at a time to calculate 3D position + time. They orbit Earth at an altitude of 11k miles twice a day
  • Frequency ranges: Broadcast radio 535–1,605 kHz (wavelength 560–187m). Communications, VOR, marker beacons 30–300MHz (wavelength=10–1m). Transponder, radar, glidescope, GPS 300–3,000 MHz (wavelength 100–10cm)
  • Indicated altitude may not reflect actual altitude: if the air is colder below the aircraft, the (unadjusted) altimeter reads higher than true altitude (aircraft is lower than indicated)
  • Watt vs kW vs hp: light bulb wattage is the amount of work required to light the bulb for one hour; 1000 watts = 1 kW = 1.3 hp; one hp could keep a 75-watt bulb lit for about an hour
  • Visibility at sea level on a clear day is about 3 SM. At 5,000 ft, visibility is about 90 miles. Earth’s curvature is visible at 50k feet where the horizon is more than 260 miles away
